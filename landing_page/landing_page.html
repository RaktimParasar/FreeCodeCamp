    What is a 8-bit Game ?

    Most video game computer microprocessors from the 1980’s were ‘8-bit’. A ‘bit’, is a short term for ‘binary digit’, a basic measuring unit for the microprocessor’s ability to process information. Home video game systems of the 80’s, and later 90’s, were often advertised by exploiting consumer ignorance to turn technical terms such as ‘8 bit’ into marketing buzzwords. 8 bit home video game consoles of the time also consisted of the Atari 2600, Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), Sega Master System and personal computers such as the Commodore 64 - such good memories!
